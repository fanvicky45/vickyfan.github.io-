01 — Business Context

Why BNPL Matters Now

Buy Now, Pay Later has rapidly scaled across e-commerce and digital payments, with Gen Z among the fastest adopters. While most discourse focuses on whether BNPL increases purchases, far fewer studies causally estimate how it affects purchase completion and basket size when controlling for consumer characteristics.

02 — Research Questions

What We're Investigating

The study is anchored by one primary question, decomposed into three testable sub-questions that align with both academic rigor and managerial relevance.

How and why does BNPL change financial spending behavior, and how can businesses use these insights to identify customer segments, improve LTV, and design future payment products aligned with System 1 and System 2 behavioral principles?

  • Does BNPL increase purchase conversion and basket size?
  • Which consumer characteristics amplify or weaken BNPL's effects?
  • Are BNPL-driven purchases sustainably profitable or likely to create later financial strain?

06 — Expected Findings

What the Evidence Predicts

Theory and prior literature predict a consistent pattern: BNPL functions as both a credit instrument and a behavioral nudge, with effects concentrated in specific segments and categories.

↑

Conversion & Basket Size

BNPL will increase purchase conversion and basket size, particularly among Gen Z shoppers exposed to instalment framing at checkout.

👗

Hedonic Category Effect

Stronger effects in fashion, beauty, accessories, and event tickets — categories tied to identity and emotional purchase motivations.

📉

Financial Literacy Moderates

Low financial literacy and high impulsivity amplify BNPL's effect. High-discipline consumers show near-zero incremental uplift.

📱

Mobile Checkout Amplifier

Mobile checkout environments amplify BNPL's behavioral nudge through reduced friction and System 1-dominant decision contexts.

⚠️

Long-Term Crowd-Out Risk

Short-term spending boosts may cause future demand crowd-out or repayment stress for financially vulnerable users.

🧠

Mental Accounting Shift

BNPL reshapes payment perception — consumers systematically underweight future instalments, distorting effective price sensitivity.

07 — Managerial Implications

Recommendations for Industry

Insights from this research help payment networks, banks, and merchants optimize BNPL deployment across the customer lifecycle — balancing conversion uplift with responsible lending practice.

1
Prioritize BNPL in mid-range hedonic categories (fashion, beauty) where incremental spending is healthy and conversion uplift is highest.
2
Avoid aggressive BNPL promotion among financially vulnerable consumers — use literacy signals and behavioral flags to gate exposure.
3
Use behavioral segmentation to target users most responsive to instalment framing, improving marketing ROI and reducing default risk.
4
Design checkout flows that balance conversion uplift with responsible lending — test friction levels via A/B experimentation.
5
For platforms like Mastercard, Shopify, and Amazon: determine when BNPL improves long-term customer LTV versus merely shifting demand forward.
